### Tip 1: Daily Dashboard Review Routine

2. New fans - Any overnight signups?
3. Content performance - What's trending?

### Tip 2: Weekly Strategy Session (30 minutes)

**Every Monday:**
- Review last week's performance
- Compare to previous week
- Identify top 3 performing posts
- Identify bottom 3 performing posts
- Plan this week's content strategy
- Set weekly revenue goal

**Questions to Ask:**
- What worked? (Do more of this)
- What didn't? (Do less or stop)
- What surprised me? (Investigate why)
- What should I test this week? (Experiment)

### Tip 3: Use Comparative Metrics

**Compare Against:**
- Your past performance (week-over-week, month-over-month)
- Your goals (are you on track?)
- Platform benchmarks (how do you stack up?)

**Don't obsess over:**
- Other creators' numbers (focus on your growth)
- Daily fluctuations (look at trends)
- Vanity metrics (views mean nothing without engagement/revenue)

### Tip 4: Correlation Analysis

**Find What Drives Results:**
- Post frequency vs. revenue
- Content type vs. engagement
- Posting time vs. views
- Price point vs. conversion

**Example Analysis:**
"I noticed when I post 5x/week (vs. 3x), my revenue increases 40%. When I post videos vs. photos, engagement is 2x higher. When I post at 8-9 PM EST, views are 60% higher than morning posts."

**Action:** More frequent video posts at 8-9 PM!

### Tip 5: Set Up Alerts

**Auto-Notifications When:**

**Benefits:**

**How to Do It:**

- "December fans spend 2x more on average - holiday shoppers!"

**Action:** Double down on what worked in high-performing months

### Technique 2: A/B Testing

**What to Test:**
- Posting times (morning vs. evening)
- Content length (2-min vs. 5-min videos)
- Price points ($5 vs. $10 PPV)
- Thumbnail styles (close-up vs. full body)
- Title formats (questions vs. statements)

**How to Test:**
1. Change ONE variable at a time
2. Run for at least 2 weeks (minimum 20 data points)
3. Compare performance metrics
4. Implement winner, test next variable

**Example:**
- Week 1-2: Post at 8 AM (track results)
- Week 3-4: Post at 8 PM (track results)
- Compare: Evening posts got 60% more views
- Action: Switch to evening posting permanently

### Technique 3: Revenue Attribution

**Understand Where Revenue Comes From:**

**By Content Type:**
- Videos: 50% of revenue
- Photos: 30% of revenue
- Galleries: 15% of revenue
- Stories: 5% of revenue

**By Revenue Source:**
- Subscriptions: 60% (most stable)
- PPV: 25% (highest per-transaction)
- Tips: 10% (appreciation)
- Messages: 5% (personal touch)

**By Fan Segment:**
- Top 10% of fans: 60% of revenue (VIPs!)
- Next 30%: 30% of revenue (regulars)
- Bottom 60%: 10% of revenue (casual)

**Action:** Focus 80% effort on top 40% of fans (Pareto Principle)

## ğŸ› Troubleshooting

### Problem: "Dashboard shows $0 revenue but I earned money"

**Cause:** Data sync delay or payment processing time

**Solution:**
1. Refresh the page (Ctrl+R or Cmd+R)
2. Check "Pending" revenue section
3. Earnings may take 24-48 hours to reflect
4. Verify in Transactions tab for real-time data
5. Contact support if still showing $0 after 48 hours

---

### Problem: "Metrics don't match what I expect"

**Cause:** Filtered date range, different time zone, or metric misunderstanding

**Solution:**
1. Check date range selector (top right)
2. Ensure "All Time" or desired period selected
3. Verify time zone settings (Settings â†’ General)
4. Read metric definitions (hover over "?" icons)
5. Compare to raw transaction data

---

### Problem: "Revenue decreased suddenly"

**Causes:**
- Subscription cancellations
- Reduced content posting
- Algorithm changes
- Increased competition
- Content policy violations

**Diagnostic Steps:**
1. Check fan churn rate - did many cancel?
2. Review recent posts - any underperformers?
3. Check moderation queue - any rejections?
4. Compare posting frequency to previous period
5. Review analytics for trend changes

**Recovery Actions:**
- Re-engage churned fans with win-back offer
- Increase posting frequency
- Create high-value PPV content
- Run limited-time promotion
- Message VIP fans personally

---

### Problem: "Charts not loading or showing errors"

**Cause:** Browser issue, ad blocker, or server problem

**Solution:**
1. Clear browser cache and cookies
2. Disable ad blockers temporarily
3. Try different browser (Chrome, Firefox recommended)
4. Check internet connection
5. Try incognito/private mode
6. Contact support if persists

## â“ Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: How often are analytics updated?
**A:** Most metrics update in real-time (within 5 minutes). Revenue data updates every 15 minutes. Complex metrics like LTV and cohort analysis update daily at midnight EST.

### Q: Can I export my analytics data?
**A:** Yes! Click "Export Data" in the top right of any analytics view. Choose format (CSV, Excel, PDF) and date range. Great for tax purposes, personal tracking, or deeper analysis in spreadsheet tools.

### Q: What's a "good" engagement rate?
**A:** 8-15% is good, >15% is excellent. Engagement rate = (Likes + Comments + Tips) Ã· Views. Higher engagement means your content resonates with your audience. Focus on engagement over raw views.

### Q: Why did my revenue spike/drop suddenly?
**A:** **Spikes:** Viral content, successful promotion, featured on platform, special event, or seasonal trend. **Drops:** Mass cancellations, reduced posting, content policy violation, increased competition, or seasonal downturn. Check Recent Activity for clues.

### Q: How do I compare my performance to other creators?
**A:** Platform provides anonymized benchmarks (see "Industry Average" on some metrics). Focus on YOUR growth over time rather than others. Every niche, audience, and creator is different. Consistent personal improvement > comparison.

### Q: Can I see which fans view my content most?
**A:** Yes! Go to Fans â†’ View Details â†’ Sort by "Most Active". You'll see fans ranked by views, engagement, and time spent on your profile. Great for identifying VIPs to give extra attention.

### Q: What's the difference between "views" and "unique viewers"?
**A:** **Views:** Total number of times content was viewed (one fan can view multiple times). **Unique Viewers:** Number of different fans who viewed. Example: If 10 fans each view your video 3 times = 30 views, 10 unique viewers.

### Q: How can I see which content generates the most revenue?
**A:** Dashboard â†’ Content Performance â†’ Sort by "Revenue". You'll see your highest-earning content. Analyze what these posts have in common (length, topic, price, timing) and create more similar content.

### Q: Why don't my dashboard numbers match my payout amount?
**A:** Dashboard shows gross revenue (before platform fee). Payouts show net amount (after platform fee and processing fees). Also, pending revenue isn't included in available payout balance until cleared (2-7 days).

### Q: Can I set custom analytics alerts?
**A:** Yes! Settings â†’ Notifications â†’ Analytics Alerts. Set thresholds for revenue goals, engagement drops, fan churn, content performance, and more. Get notified via email, push notification, or in-app alert.
