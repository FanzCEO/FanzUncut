## <a name="manual-review"></a>üë§ Manual Review Process

### When Content Requires Human Review

Your content enters manual review if:
- AI confidence score is below 85%
- Multiple user reports received (3+)
- Flagged keywords detected
- You're a new creator (first 5 uploads)
- Content contains borderline material

### Review Timeline

| Priority | Typical Review Time | Examples |
|----------|---------------------|----------|
| **Critical** | 1-4 hours | Reports of illegal content |
| **High** | 4-12 hours | Policy violation flags |
| **Normal** | 12-24 hours | New creator uploads |
| **Low** | 24-48 hours | Appeals, reclassifications |

### What Happens During Review?

**Step 1: Queue**
- Content enters moderation queue
- You see "Under Review" status
- Content is **not visible** to fans yet

**Step 2: Human Moderator Reviews**
- Checks against community guidelines
- Verifies AI scan accuracy
- Reviews context and intent
- Makes decision: Approve, Reject, or Request Changes

**Step 3: Notification**
- You receive decision via notification
- Email sent with details
- If rejected: Reason provided + option to appeal

**Step 4: Action**
- **Approved:** Content goes live immediately
- **Rejected:** Content deleted, penalties may apply
- **Changes Requested:** Edit and resubmit

### How to Check Review Status

```
Dashboard ‚Üí Content Health ‚Üí "In Review" tab
```

You'll see:
- Number of items in review
- Estimated review time remaining
- Notification when review is complete

## <a name="appeals"></a>‚öñÔ∏è Appeal Process

### When to Appeal

Appeal a moderation decision if:
- You believe the decision was incorrect
- You can provide additional context or evidence
- The content complies with guidelines but was misunderstood
- Technical error occurred in automated scanning

**Don't appeal if:**
- Content clearly violates policies (wastes time, may hurt standing)
- You want to argue about the rules themselves
- Appeal is just emotional venting

### How to File an Appeal

**Step 1: Gather Evidence**
- Screenshots of content
- Consent forms or model releases
- ID verification documents
- Timestamps or metadata
- Previous approvals of similar content

**Step 2: Submit Appeal**

1. Go to **Support** ‚Üí **Appeals**
2. Click **"File New Appeal"**
3. Fill out appeal form:
   - **Content ID:** (found in original moderation notice)
   - **Original Decision:** (e.g., "Content Removed - Policy Violation")
   - **Reason for Appeal:** (select from dropdown)
   - **Your Explanation:** (detailed, factual explanation)
   - **Supporting Evidence:** (upload documents/screenshots)
4. Submit

**Step 3: Wait for Review**
- Appeals reviewed within 3-5 business days
- You'll receive email notification of decision
- Decision is **final** (no secondary appeals)

### Appeal Outcomes

| Outcome | What It Means | Next Steps |
|---------|---------------|------------|
| **Appeal Granted** | Original decision overturned | Content restored, strike removed |
| **Appeal Partially Granted** | Content approved with changes | Edit content per guidance, resubmit |
| **Appeal Denied** | Original decision upheld | Content remains removed, strike stands |

### Writing a Strong Appeal

**Bad Appeal Example:**
```
This is BS! My content didn't violate anything. You guys always
target creators like me. I want my content restored NOW or I'm
leaving this platform!

3. Content was reviewed and approved by me and participants before posting

The "struggle" theme in the video is fantasy roleplay, clearly

This content complies with Section 4.3 of Content Guidelines
regarding consensual adult roleplay.

I believe this was incorrectly flagged by automated scanning and

Thank you for your time.

Attachments:
- Consent Form - Participant 1.pdf

```

### For Fans: Responsible Reporting

**When to Report:**
- You genuinely believe content violates policies
- You see prohibited content (illegal, non-consensual, minors)
- Creator is impersonating someone else
- Content was stolen from another creator

**When NOT to Report:**
- You simply don't like the content (just unsubscribe)

- You're trying to silence someone you disagree with

**How to Report Responsibly:**

3. One report per issue (don't spam multiple reports)

## <a name="troubleshooting"></a>üîß Troubleshooting

### "My content is stuck in review for 48+ hours"

**Solutions:**
1. Check **Content Health Dashboard** for queue status
2. Peak times (weekends, holidays) have longer waits
3. If >72 hours, contact support with Content ID
4. Provide any additional context that might speed review

### "Content was auto-rejected but I don't know why"

**Solutions:**
1. Check moderation notice email for specific reason
2. Use **Pre-Upload Checker** to identify issues
3. Common causes:
   - Participants appear under 18 (even if they're not)
   - Prohibited keywords in title/description
   - Background music copyright claim
   - Watermark from another platform visible
4. Edit and resubmit, or file appeal with evidence

### "I received a strike but don't agree with it"

**Solutions:**
1. **Read the specific policy cited** in the strike notice
2. If you believe it's an error, **file an appeal** with evidence
3. If appeal is denied, accept it and move forward
4. **Learn from it:** Avoid similar content in future
5. Strikes expire after 90 days of good standing

### "My account was suspended"

**Solutions:**
1. Check email for suspension notice (includes reason and duration)
2. Suspensions are typically:
   - **1st offense:** 7-day suspension
   - **2nd offense:** 30-day suspension
   - **3rd offense:** Permanent ban
3. You **cannot post or earn** during suspension
4. You **can** appeal suspensions within 5 days
5. If appeal denied, serve the suspension period
6. Account reinstated automatically after suspension ends

### "Fan is harassing me with false reports"

**Solutions:**
1. Document the pattern (screenshot reports, dates)
2. Contact support: "Harassment via False Reports"
3. Provide evidence of pattern
4. Our system will investigate their report history
5. If confirmed malicious, they'll be penalized
6. You can block users from viewing/reporting your content

## <a name="faq"></a>‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long does automated scanning take?**
A: Usually 10-60 seconds depending on file size. Videos take longer than photos.

**Q: Can I see what the AI scanner detects?**
A: Yes, click on any uploaded content ‚Üí "View Moderation Report" to see detailed scan results.

**Q: What happens if I get 3 strikes?**
A: 3 strikes = permanent account termination. Strikes expire after 90 days of good standing.

**Q: Can I remove a strike by deleting the content?**
A: No. Once a strike is issued, deleting the content doesn't remove it. You can appeal, or wait 90 days for it to expire.

**Q: Is there a way to "pre-verify" risky content?**
A: Yes! Use the Pre-Upload Checker (Settings ‚Üí Content Tools) to scan content before uploading.

**Q: What if a fan reports my content out of spite?**
A: All reports are reviewed by humans. False reports won't result in penalties. Repeated false reporting by the fan will get THEM suspended.

**Q: Can I appeal a permanent ban?**
A: Permanent bans cannot be appealed in most cases. Only if you can prove account compromise (hacking) or severe technical error.

**Q: Are my private messages moderated?**
A: Private DMs are only reviewed if reported or if automated scanning detects illegal content (CSAM, trafficking, etc.).

**Q: What if I accidentally uploaded prohibited content?**
A: Delete it immediately and contact support to explain. Honest mistakes are treated differently than intentional violations.

**Q: How do I prove consent for roleplay content?**
A: Keep signed consent forms, email/text confirmations from participants, and ideally record a brief "consent check-in" at the start of filming.

**Q: Can I disagree with a specific policy?**
A: You can provide feedback via Support ‚Üí "Policy Feedback", but you must comply with current policies while using the platform.

**Q: What's the difference between a "warning" and a "strike"?**
A: **Warning** = Verbal caution, no penalty, good for first-time minor issues. **Strike** = Formal violation on your record, counts toward suspension/ban.

**Q: How do I check my account standing?**
A: Dashboard ‚Üí Content Health ‚Üí See your standing badge (Green, Yellow, Orange, Red, or Black).

**Q: Can I delete my moderation history?**
A: No. All moderation actions are permanent records. However, strikes expire after 90 days.

**Q: What if someone is stealing and reposting my content?**
A: File a DMCA takedown report (Support ‚Üí Copyright ‚Üí File DMCA). Provide proof of ownership. We typically respond within 48 hours.
